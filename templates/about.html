St. Teresa's College (Autonomous) 🔗

Founded in 1925, St. Teresa’s College (Autonomous), Ernakulam is one of Kerala’s premier institutions for women’s education, celebrating a century of academic excellence and women’s empowerment in 2025. Over the past 100 years, the college has nurtured generations of confident, competent, and socially committed women from diverse backgrounds.

The institution is recognized for its holistic approach to education, combining academic excellence with value-based learning, personal growth, leadership development, and community engagement. Through innovative curriculum design, research-oriented learning, and student-centered pedagogy, the college continues to adapt to the evolving demands of higher education and society.

As a distinguished centre of learning, St. Teresa’s College remains committed to fostering intellectual curiosity, social responsibility, and inclusive growth while shaping future leaders equipped to contribute meaningfully to society and nation-building.

Department of Botany

Established in 1957, the Department of Botany at St. Teresa's College is one of the oldest and most prestigious departments of the institution. Offering undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral programmes, the department is recognized for its academic excellence, research contributions, and commitment to holistic education.

The department holds the distinction of producing the first Ph.D. in Botany under Mahatma Gandhi University and has awarded 32 doctoral degrees to date. With advanced laboratories, ICT-enabled classrooms, a botanical museum, botanical garden, department library, and the internationally indexed Dr. Sr. Avita Herbarium (DSAH) housing over 8,800 specimens, the department provides a dynamic environment for teaching, research, and innovation in botanical sciences.

Dr. Sr. Avita Herbarium (DSAH)

The Herbarium housed at the Department of Botany has long served as an integral centre for botanical research, teaching, and biodiversity documentation within the department. In 2024, the herbarium received official recognition through the allotment of its acronym, the Dr. Sr. Avita Herbarium (DSAH), and was indexed in the Index Herbariorum, marking an important milestone in its development and academic significance.

The herbarium houses more than 8,800 preserved plant specimens representing the rich and diverse flora of the Western Ghats, one of the world’s biodiversity hotspots. A significant portion of the collection has been contributed by Dr. Sr. Avita, whose efforts have greatly enriched the repository.

DSAH actively supports taxonomic studies, academic learning, and scientific documentation, while continuing to strengthen initiatives in herbarium digitization, specimen curation, and biodiversity data management. Through its growing collections and research support, the herbarium contributes to the preservation and understanding of India’s plant diversity.
